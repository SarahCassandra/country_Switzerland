DESTINATIONS

GRINDELWALD

Overview:
Grindelwald is a cozy alpine village in the Bernese Oberland, surrounded by the dramatic peaks of the Eiger. It offers both peaceful scenery and exciting mountain adventures, making it a perfect destination all year round.

Highlights:

  • Eiger North Face
  • First Cliff Walk by Tissot
  • Panoramic hiking trails
  • Cable car mountain viewpoints

Best Time to Visit:
Summer (June–September) for hiking and lake trails
Winter (December–March) for skiing and snow adventures

Top Things To Do:

  • Take the cable car to Grindelwald First
  • Try the First Flyer zip line
  • Hike to Bachalpsee Lake
  • Enjoy skiing or snowboarding in winter

Recommended Stay: 2–3 days

BERN

Overview:
Bern, the capital of Switzerland, is known for its medieval charm and beautifully preserved Old Town, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Its riverside views, cobblestone streets, and relaxed pace make it a warm and welcoming city.

Highlights:

  • The Zytglogge Clock Tower
  • Bear Park
  • Federal Palace
  • Aare River viewpoints

Best Time to Visit:
May to September for pleasant walking weather

Top Things To Do:

  • Walk along the Old Town arcade
  • Watch the astronomical clock show
  • Enjoy sunset by the Aare River
  • Visit the Einstein House

Recommended Stay: 1–2 days

ENGELBERG

Overview:
Engelberg is a picturesque village surrounded by towering mountains and peaceful green valleys. It's the home of Mount Titlis, known for its breath-taking glacier views and rotating cable car experience.

Highlights:

  • Mount Titlis
  • Cliff Walk Suspension Bridge
  • Alpine lakes and forests

Best Time to Visit: Year-round, especially December–April for snow activities

Top Things To Do:

  • Ride the Titlis Rotair cable car
  • Walk on the Titlis Glacier Bridge
  • Enjoy scenic hiking or biking

Recommended Stay: 1–2 days

ZÜRICH

Overview:
Zürich blends modern sophistication with historical elegance. It's the cultural and financial heart of Switzerland, sitting peacefully beside the clear waters of Lake Zürich.

Highlights:

  • Old Town (Altstadt)
  • Lake Zürich promenades
  • Bahnhofstrasse shopping street

Best Time to Visit: May to September for lakeside walks and boat trips

Top Things To Do:

  • Take a boat cruise on Lake Zürich
  • Explore Niederdorf old town alleys
  • Visit art museums like Kunsthaus Zürich

Recommended Stay: 1–3 days

LUCERNE

Overview:
Lucerne is surrounded by breathtaking scenery, combining medieval charm with calm lakeside beauty. It is one of Switzerland’s most scenic and romantic destinations.

Highlights:

  • Chapel Bridge
  • Lion Monument
  • Lake Lucerne
  • Mount Pilatus & Rigi nearby

Top Things To Do:

  • Walk along the Chapel Bridge
  • Take a lake cruise
  • Ride the cable car to Mt. Pilatus

Recommended Stay: 1–2 days

GENEVA

Overview:
Geneva is a refined lakeside city known as an international diplomatic center. It blends nature, culture, and global influence beautifully.

Highlights:

  • Jet d’Eau Fountaine
  • United Nations Headquarters
  • Lake Geneva promenade

Top Things To Do:

  • Visit the UN Palace
  • Explore the waterfront parks
  • Take a boat to Montreux or Lausanne

Recommended Stay: 1–2 days

JUNGFRAUJOCH – “TOP OF EUROPE”

Overview:
Jungfraujoch is one of the highest accessible points in Europe and offers striking snow views all year round. It is truly a once-in-a-lifetime experience.

Highlights:

  • Ice Palace
  • Sphinx Observatory
  • Panoramic glacier views

Recommended Stay: Half-day visit from Grindelwald or Lauterbrunnen

LAUTERBRUNNEN

Overview:
Lauterbrunnen sits in a stunning valley surrounded by 72 waterfalls. The landscape feels fairytale-like and serene, perfect for photography and nature walks.

Highlights:

  • Staubbach Falls
  • Trümmelbach Falls
  • Access to Wengen and Mürren

Recommended Stay:1-2 days
